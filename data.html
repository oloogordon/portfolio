Data Validation

  • Format validation: Names, Numbers, Dates, Location standards were observed in German, UK and South African projects
  • Type Check: data type checks was practised in all projects each day during reporting in google sheets
  • Range Check 1: Census data were cross checked to ensure dates fell within the original census dates
  • Range Check 2: Age was cross checked to match the difference between birth date and the census date and miscalculations were corrected
  • Stastistical validation: Margin of error was observed for a certain range and anything over was flagged for review and second opinion
  • Pattern matcing: Names were validated using surname patterns in the UK census project
  • Data profiling: Ther germany project contained strict validating policies and various entried were compared with others afterwhich the marriage record was eiher approved or rejected and notification raised
  • Entry/Prescence Check: in the South african childrens project and the US curation project entry checks were part of the process before record submistion to ensure entries are not empty
  • Data validation principles (pre-entry, entry, post entry validation) were practised in all three projects
  • Aim: Improved quality, Error prevention, enhanced data consistency, data integrity, faster processing, compliancy

Data Processing Plan

Before the begining of each projects, Clear objectives were defined, inc proper and convinient methods of access and reporting of data was establised.
